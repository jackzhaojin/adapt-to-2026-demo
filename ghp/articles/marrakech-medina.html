Lost in the Marrakech Medina

by Katie · Posted October 9, 2002 · Marrakech, Morocco
One of a thousand identical-and-not-identical alleys.

I'd like to tell you I navigated the old city of Marrakech like a seasoned traveller. The truth is I got completely, hopelessly lost within about ten minutes and stayed lost for most of three days, and it was one of the best things that's ever happened to me.

The maze is the point

The medina is a tangle of narrow alleys that twist back on themselves, with no street signs and a paper map that is basically a work of fiction. Every turn opens onto something — a spice pyramid, a man hammering a copper pot, a cart of mint, a cat asleep on a stack of rugs. You stop trying to get somewhere and just follow your nose.

Learning to bargain

I am a polite midwestern girl and haggling made me want to crawl out of my skin. But in the souk it's expected, even friendly, and refusing to play is almost rude. My first attempt at a leather bag was a disaster; by the third day I'd talked a teapot down to a third of the opening price and the seller shook my hand and gave me a free glass of tea.

Heads up: Friendly young guys will offer to "show you the way" and then demand money, or walk you to their cousin's shop. A polite "la, shukran" (no, thanks) and confidence go a long way. And agree on a price BEFORE anyone wraps anything.

Djemaa el-Fna at night

Every evening the big square fills with food stalls, drummers, storytellers and smoke, and the whole thing feels like it's been happening exactly this way for five hundred years. I ate skewers at a plastic table, got slightly ripped off, didn't care at all, and watched the sky go orange behind the Koutoubia minaret.

Marrakech overwhelmed every one of my senses and I'd go back tomorrow. Just don't expect the map to help you. Let yourself get lost — that's the trip.
