Cycling the Loire Valley

by Katie · Posted May 17, 2003 · Loire Valley, France
You round a bend and there's just... a castle. Casually.

After a few hardcore trips I wanted something gentle, and the Loire Valley delivered. The idea: rent a bike, follow the river, and pedal between fairy-tale chateaux with nothing heavier than a baguette and a wedge of cheese in the basket. Reader, it was as nice as it sounds.

The bike (and its wobbly wheel)

I rented a slightly elderly touring bike in Tours for a few euros a day. The back wheel had a wobble that I named "Gerard" and we made our peace by day two. The "Loire à Vélo" route is mostly flat, well signed, and runs along quiet lanes and old towpaths, so even a casual cyclist like me could manage 30–40 km a day with plenty of stops.

Chateaux on a budget

You don't have to pay to get into every castle (the entry fees add up fast). Half the joy is the approach — Chenonceau arching over its river, Amboise above the rooftops, Villandry's gardens visible over the wall. I picked one to actually tour properly and admired the rest from a picnic blanket with my cheese.

Katie's tips: Buy lunch in the morning before the shops shut for their long midday break, or you'll be hungry and indignant at 1pm like I was. Carry a spare inner tube. And the regional trains take bikes, so you can cheat the headwind home.

The good life, briefly

Most evenings ended in a tiny village with a campsite or a cheap chambre d'hôte, a glass of local wine, and legs pleasantly tired. After the chaos of Marrakech and the thin air of Peru, freewheeling past vineyards with the sun out felt like the easiest kind of happiness. Sometimes the gentle trip is the one you remember most.
