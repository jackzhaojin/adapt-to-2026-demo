Prague Hostels on $10 a Day

by Katie · Posted August 23, 2002 · Prague, Czech Republic
Old Town from the hostel window. Not bad for $4 a night.

People kept telling me Prague was the most beautiful city in Europe AND one of the cheapest, and for once the internet was right on both counts. I spent a week here and kept a strict little notebook of every koruna I spent, because a few of you e-mailed asking how I do it on basically no money. So here is the actual math.

The daily budget

This was 2002 and the koruna was very kind to a backpacker. Here's a typical day, converted roughly to US dollars:

ItemCost (USD)
Hostel dorm bed (10-bed)$4.00
Bakery breakfast + coffee$1.20
Lunch (street sausage + bread)$1.50
Dinner (goulash at a pub)$2.50
Tram ticket$0.40
Total$9.60

How to keep it that low

The trick is that the expensive stuff in Prague is also the stuff you don't need. The castle grounds, the Charles Bridge, the Old Town Square, the astronomical clock doing its hourly thing — all free to just stand around and gawk at, which is 90% of why you came. I splurged exactly once, on a classical concert in a tiny church, and it was worth breaking budget for.

Katie's tips: Eat where the construction workers eat at lunch — the "polední menu" set lunches are huge and cheap. Carry coins for the trams, inspectors are real and the fine is not fun. And learn to say "dobrý den" (hello), it changes every interaction.

I left Prague genuinely sad, which doesn't happen to me often. If you're doing a Europe trip on fumes, put this city near the start so it spoils you early.
