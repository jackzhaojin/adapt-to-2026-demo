What gear do I need for the Cathedral Lakes loop?

Start with the Ten Essentials: navigation (map & compass), sun protection, insulation, illumination, a first-aid kit, fire, a repair kit, nutrition, hydration, and emergency shelter.

  • Moisture-wicking wool or synthetic layers — never cotton
  • High-traction boots with ankle support
  • A lightweight rain shell, even on bluebird days

How hard is the climb to Forester Pass?

We rate trails on elevation gain, terrain technicality, and distance. Forester earns a Strenuous rating: over 1,000 ft of gain per mile near the top, with exposed switchbacks above 13,000 ft.

Allow one hour for every two miles, plus an extra hour for every 1,000 ft of climbing.

When is the best season to hike the high country?

Late summer through early autumn (September–October) is the peak window. The air is crisp, the crowds have thinned, and the biting insects of early summer have vanished.

Always check conditions — spring "mud season" brings erosion and dangerous stream crossings.

Are dogs allowed on the trail?

Most national forests and state parks are dog-friendly, though leash laws vary. National Parks typically restrict dogs to roads and campgrounds to protect native wildlife.

Wherever you roam: keep them leashed, pack out waste, and give wildlife a wide berth.

How do I get a wilderness permit?

Permits for the Sierra high country are released on a rolling six-month window through Recreation.gov. Popular trailheads like Whitney Portal sell out within minutes — set a reminder and have your itinerary ready.

Is there water along the route?

Through July, snowmelt keeps most creeks flowing. By late season, plan your camps around reliable lakes and always carry a filter. We mark dependable sources on every trail log.

What about bears?

A bear canister is required above the treeline in most Sierra zones. Store all scented items — food, sunscreen, toothpaste — and never leave your pack unattended at a viewpoint.

Can I camp anywhere I like?

Practice Leave No Trace: camp on durable surfaces at least 100 ft from water, use established sites where they exist, and keep group sizes small to protect fragile alpine meadows.
