Caught in the Kerala Monsoon

by Katie · Posted July 6, 2003 · Kerala, India
It rained like this for hours. Then it stopped. Then it did it again.

I will be honest with you: I planned this trip badly. I booked Kerala for July because the flights were cheap, and the flights were cheap because it is the middle of monsoon season. Everyone I told made a face. And it turned into the most beautiful mistake of my whole year on the road.

Rain like I'd never seen

The monsoon isn't a drizzle you wait out. It arrives like someone tipping a lake over, warm and total, and then it just... stops, and the light goes electric green. The whole landscape — rice paddies, palms, tea hills — looks impossibly saturated, like the color knob got turned all the way up.

The backwaters

I splurged (by my standards) on a night on a houseboat in the Alleppey backwaters. We drifted through narrow channels past villages where life happens on the water, and the rain drummed on the palm-thatch roof while I drank chai and felt like the richest broke person alive. It cost less than a cheap hotel back home.

Katie's tips: Embrace the wet — a cheap umbrella and quick-dry everything beats a useless rain jacket in that heat. Hotel rates are slashed in monsoon, so you can afford nicer places. And try the "Kerala meals" served on a banana leaf, it's the best $1 you'll spend.

Off-season magic

Because hardly any tourists come in July, I had places to myself that are mobbed in winter. Tea plantation roads, empty. Temples, quiet. People had time to actually talk to me. I came for cheap flights and left convinced that the "wrong" season is sometimes exactly the right one. Just pack for water.
