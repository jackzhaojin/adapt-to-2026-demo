Diving the Reef on a Shoestring

by Katie · Posted November 11, 2003 · Cairns, Queensland, Australia
My disposable underwater camera did NOT do this justice.

For ages I assumed scuba diving was a rich-person hobby and crossed it off my list. Then I got to Cairns, the backpacker capital of Australia, and discovered that getting your open-water certification on the Great Barrier Reef is almost a rite of passage out here, and it doesn't have to wreck your budget.

Getting certified cheap

I paid for my reception shifts at the hostel partly in dive-course credit, which is a very Cairns thing to be able to do. A four-day PADI course there cost a fraction of what it would back home, and it included two days of pool/theory and then two days out on the actual reef. I was terrified of the theory exam and aced it, mostly out of stubbornness.

First open-water dive

Rolling backwards off the boat into open ocean for the first time, my brain was sure I was about to die. Then I looked down. The reef just unfolds beneath you in colors that don't seem real — coral cities full of clownfish, parrotfish, a reef shark cruising past that the instructor was thrilled about and I was less thrilled about. Then a sea turtle drifted by at arm's length, totally unbothered, and I forgot to be scared.

Be careful: Don't book the very cheapest operator without checking their safety record — this is one place where the bottom-dollar option isn't worth it. And never touch the coral or the wildlife; it's fragile and some of it can hurt you right back.

Worth every cent

That certification card is the souvenir I'm proudest of. It turned the ocean from something I looked at into somewhere I could go. If you make it to the Queensland coast with a few spare days, do the course — broke backpacker or not, you'll find a way to afford it, and you'll be so glad you did.
